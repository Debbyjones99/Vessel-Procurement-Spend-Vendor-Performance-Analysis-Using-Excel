## Project Overview

This project analyzes procurement activities for vessel operations using a real-world company procurement dataset. The analysis was carried out entirely in Microsoft Excel to understand spending patterns, vendor performance, procurement trends, and recurring operational purchases.

The project focuses on identifying procurement inefficiencies, monitoring operational spending, and uncovering opportunities for cost reduction through better purchasing strategies and vendor management.

## Business Objectives

* Analyze month-on-month procurement spend
* Evaluate vendor performance and procurement concentration
* Assess procured items
* Identify drivers of high procurement spending
* Detect frequently requested or excess procurement items
* Monitor overall spend trends over time
* Recommend cost optimization opportunities

## Key Findings
* The highest procurement spend recorded was ₦3,044,140, associated with vendor Lubserv for the purchase of Lube Oil.
* Procurement spend increased significantly in October, recording approximately 362% growth compared to the previous month, with a total spend of ₦3,625,640.
* The most frequently purchased item category was Filters, with approximately 176 filter-related units procured across different filter types.
* Operational consumables such as medications, cleaning materials, rags, and engine requisition items appeared repeatedly throughout the procurement cycle.

### Key Observations
1. Frequent Purchases of Filters and Lube Oil
The analysis revealed repeated procurement of engineering consumables such as filters and lube oil, indicating:

* High operational dependency on these items
* Potential inventory monitoring gaps
* Opportunities for bulk purchasing strategies

2. Recurring Operational Consumables

Items such as:

* Medications
* Cleaning materials
* Rags
* Engine requisition materials
* Deck requisition supplies

Were frequently requested, suggesting the need for better inventory planning and stock management.

3. Vendor Concentration

The dataset showed repeated procurement from certain vendors over time. Heavy dependence on a limited number of vendors may:

* Reduce negotiation power
* Increase procurement costs
* Limit competitive pricing opportunities

## Recommendations
Improve Inventory Monitoring

Implement a structured inventory tracking process for fast-moving operational items through:

* Daily onboard usage reporting
* Periodic stock verification
* Better procurement approval monitoring
* Adopt Bulk Purchasing Strategies

Frequently requested operational items should be procured in bulk where practical to:

* Reduce repetitive purchases
* Improve vendor negotiation power
* Lower operational procurement costs
* Strengthen Vendor Price Evaluation
* Negotiate volume-based discounts with recurring vendors
* Compare supplier pricing regularly
* Monitor vendor performance and procurement efficiency
